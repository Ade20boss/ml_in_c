01 — The Problem

Your phone is smart.
But it's working
for someone else.

01

AI breaks your flow

Every AI tool — ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot — forces you to stop, copy, switch tabs, paste, read, then switch back. That loop destroys concentration. It interrupts what you were doing and makes you rebuild context from scratch every single time.

02

Sensitive data leaks on calls

Millions share their screens daily on Zoom and WhatsApp, accidentally exposing bank balances, passwords, and private messages. There is no automatic protection. You have to remember to minimize. Most of the time, you don't.

03

Knowledge evaporates

After two hours of research across multiple tabs, you have nothing unless you manually copy and organize notes. 70% of what you read is forgotten within 24 hours. The work of understanding disappears the moment you close your browser.

04

Your phone optimizes against you

Every app you use is optimizing for its own goals using your behavior as raw material. Instagram maximizes time-on-platform. News apps maximize anxiety-driven refreshes. You have no mechanism to make your phone work for your goals instead. Until now.

02 — The Core Mechanic

Three things happen
every time you hover.

01

Read

Dwell detects exactly what your cursor rests on — a paragraph, an error code, a form field, a price, a bank balance, a date — and reads its full context from the operating system's native accessibility layer. This happens in milliseconds. Invisibly. Without any action from you. The same layer powers all nine features simultaneously.

02

Understand

A small AI model — running entirely on your device, never touching the internet — determines what the content means, what category it falls into, what your goals are in this moment, and what action would be most useful. No servers. No latency. No data leaving your phone or laptop. Your screen content stays on your screen.

03

Act

A small, transparent overlay appears directly on your screen with exactly what you need. You read it. Dismiss it. Continue exactly where you were. No new window. No app switch. No friction. The entire interaction takes under two seconds and leaves your workflow completely intact.

03 — The Nine Features

An ambient intelligence
suite built around you.

Feature I

All users · All devices

Dwell Lens

Hover over any raw data — an error code, a timestamp, a JSON blob, an IBAN, a hex color, a phone number — and Dwell instantly transforms it into something useful and readable. An error code becomes a plain-English diagnosis. A timestamp becomes a human-readable date. A JSON object becomes a summary. No copy, no Google, no tab switch. Every transformation happens in place, in under a second. Runs on the 135M model — available on every device Dwell supports.

Feature II

All users · All devices

Dwell Veil

Automatically detects sensitive data near your cursor — bank balances, passwords, OTPs, card numbers, private chat messages, account details — and masks them the moment a screen recording or video call is detected. You never have to remember to minimize a window again. For Nigerian users: your GTBank balance, your OPay wallet, your WhatsApp conversations — protected automatically the moment you share your screen with anyone. Uses regex matching on entry devices and full model inference on mid-range and above.

Feature III

All users · All devices

Dwell Focus

Tracks cursor dwell time and gently dims everything on screen outside the region you are actively reading or working in. A soft visual tunnel forms around your attention. Zero hotkeys. Zero mode switching. Zero configuration. It activates and deactivates by itself based purely on where you already are. Works simultaneously across every open app. Requires no AI inference at all — pure cursor geometry and overlay rendering. Available on every device.

Feature IV

Pro & Max · Mid-range+

Dwell Compass

Every app on your phone optimizes for its own goals using your behavior as raw material. Dwell Compass lets you define what your digital life actually optimizes for — and enforces it. You write your objective in plain language: "I want to feel mentally sharp and make progress on what I'm building." Dwell translates this into measurable behavioral proxies, scores your phone use against your own goals in real time, and surfaces a daily alignment curve. Over time, it identifies exactly which behaviors move your score up and which move it down — based on your patterns, not a generic model.

Feature V

Pro & Max · Mid-range+

Dwell Forge

Most people plateaued at their reading comprehension level in secondary school. Dwell Forge trains you to read better — invisibly, inside every piece of content you already read, without adding any new behavior. A pre-read prime activates before you start. A retrieval prompt appears 12 minutes after you finish, timed to the cognitive science optimal window. When you respond, Forge tells you exactly what you captured correctly, names the single most important thing you missed, and asks one question that extends your thinking. Over 30 sessions it surfaces your specific comprehension patterns. Over a year your reading is measurably different.

Feature VI

Pro & Max · Mid-range+

Dwell Memory

Everything you hover on for more than two seconds gets silently logged with surrounding context. After two hours of research across multiple tabs, Dwell produces a structured, plain-English summary — what you read, what conflicted with what, and what topics you missed — without you ever touching a notes app. One tap exports the entire brief to WhatsApp. Includes Contradiction Flag: a quiet alert when two things you've read in the same session directly contradict each other. Local storage with user-defined retention. Nothing leaves the device.

Feature VII

Pro & Max · Mid-range+

Dwell Pulse

Built specifically for Nigerian university students. As you read textbooks and lecture notes, Dwell cross-references the content against a local database of past WAEC, JAMB, NECO, and university exam questions using semantic similarity matching — not just keyword search. A subtle indicator appears on high-frequency exam topics. The night before an exam, Dwell generates a full priority-ranked Crammer's Brief based on what you've actually read versus what past papers test most heavily. One tap sends the plan to your WhatsApp study group. Physical past question booklets sell for ₦2,000 per subject. Dwell Pulse covers every subject for ₦3,500 a month.

Feature VIII

Max · Mid-range+ · Requires internet

Dwell Search

The only feature that touches the network — and only partially. When you need information beyond your screen, Dwell Search sends your query to the Brave Search API, retrieves the top five results as titles and snippets, then synthesizes a single coherent answer locally using the on-device model. The search query leaves your device. Your screen content never does. Sources are cited. You can tap any result to open it in your browser. This is the first AI search experience that costs nothing in server inference because all the intelligence happens on your phone.

Feature IX — The Signature Feature

Max · High-end only · 8GB+ RAM

Dwell Live

Your AI that sees your screen and hears your room at the same time.

Every other AI tool is blind and deaf. It only knows what you type into it. Dwell Live changes the entire equation. It reads your screen and listens to audio simultaneously — combining both streams to understand what is actually happening around you in real time, without you doing anything. This is the demo that makes people pull out their wallets.

In a lecture: Dwell reads the slides on screen and transcribes what the professor says. At the end of class, a structured set of notes is ready — combining what was on the slides with what was explained verbally, with high-frequency exam topics already flagged by Dwell Pulse. The student took zero notes. They just attended the lecture.

In a meeting: Dwell reads the shared screen and transcribes the discussion simultaneously. At the end: action items, decisions made, who said what, and a one-paragraph executive summary — automatically. You look like the most prepared person in every meeting without preparing anything.

The technology: Whisper.cpp — open source, production-quality — runs locally for speech recognition via system audio capture APIs. Combined with the existing screen reader and the 4B on-device model, Dwell Live adds zero server costs and is available only on 8GB+ devices where the computational headroom exists to run it reliably.

04 — Technical Architecture

Local-first.
Privacy by design,
not by policy.

No screen content ever leaves the device. This is not a privacy policy claim — it is an architectural fact. The AI model is embedded inside the app itself. There is no server processing what users see. The one exception is Dwell Search, which sends only the search query — never the screen content — to the Brave Search API.

[ LAYER 01 ]

The Eyes — C++ Core Engine

A native C++ engine hooks into each operating system's accessibility layer. On Android: the Accessibility Service via a thin ~80-line JNI bridge. On Windows: Win32 API with low-level mouse hooks. On Linux: AT-SPI2 bus over X11. It reads the UI element under the cursor with millisecond latency, feeds it to every active feature simultaneously.

[ LAYER 02 ]

The Router — Hardware Intelligence

At startup, Dwell probes available device RAM and assigns the device to Tier A, B, or C. The appropriate model loads automatically. A ₦60,000 Tecno phone runs a different model than a flagship Samsung — but the features available on that device always run at the best quality that device can support. Capability degrades gracefully, never crashes.

[ LAYER 03 ]

The Brain — On-Device AI

Open-source models run via llama.cpp (text understanding) and Whisper.cpp (voice, for Dwell Live). All models are 4-bit quantized to minimize RAM footprint. SmolLM2 135M uses ~350MB. Qwen3 1.7B uses ~1.5GB. Qwen3 4B uses ~3GB. Each tier unlocks progressively more powerful features while running entirely offline.

[ LAYER 04 ]

The Bridge — Android JNI

On Android, approximately 80 lines of Java wrap the OS Accessibility Service and pass events directly to the C++ engine. All logic lives in C. The Java layer is a doorman — it receives the knock and opens the door. Nothing more. This design means the entire core is portable to any platform that supports the C ABI.

[ LAYER 05 ]

The Canvas — Overlay UI

A transparent, always-on-top canvas renders Dwell's responses directly over any active application without opening a new window. GPU-accelerated, lightweight, dismisses on any touch or click. For Dwell Forge: a persistent sidebar session. For Dwell Compass: an ambient indicator. For Dwell Live: a live structured document that scrolls as the session progresses.

[ LAYER 06 ]

The Backend — Featherlight

The only server component handles authentication, subscription status, and the Brave Search API proxy for Dwell Search. Hosted on Supabase free tier and Railway free tier. Monthly cost: under $20 regardless of user count. No AI computation happens here. No screen content passes through here. Ever.

07 — Competitive Position

Seven structural advantages
nobody can replicate fast.

Privacy is architectural, not a promise

The AI runs entirely on-device. No screen content ever leaves the phone. A competitor using cloud AI cannot structurally make this claim — they would have to demolish and rebuild their entire infrastructure. This moat deepens over time, not weakens.

Near-zero server costs — forever

Because AI runs locally, infrastructure costs stay under $20/month regardless of how many users we have. A competitor running equivalent cloud AI would spend $50,000–$500,000 per month at scale. Our unit economics are structurally superior from day one and improve as hardware gets cheaper.

Built for affordable phones, by design

Dwell Lens, Veil, and Focus run fully on a ₦60,000 Tecno phone with 4GB of RAM. Most AI products require premium hardware or a cloud subscription. We serve the billions that every Western AI company ignores. That is our base market before we expand upward to power users.

Dwell Pulse has no global competitor — and never will

No international product will build WAEC and JAMB-specific exam intelligence for Nigerian students. It requires local knowledge, local database curation, and local trust. This is our moat in the proving-ground market, producing users with genuine emotional loyalty before we go global.

Dwell Compass cannot be built by any ad-supported platform

The optimization function feature — which shows users exactly how far their phone use is from their own stated goals — is structurally impossible for Instagram, TikTok, or any platform whose revenue depends on maximizing the same engagement that Compass identifies as misaligned. Only a product with no advertising model can offer this feature honestly.

Dwell Forge compounds with time

The cognitive training effect of Dwell Forge grows with every session. A user who has used Forge for 6 months has a measurably different reading comprehension profile than one who hasn't. This creates structural retention — leaving Dwell means stopping a training regimen that has been running invisibly for months. Churn becomes progressively harder as the product embeds itself in the user's intellectual development.

Zero behavior change required

Every other AI product requires learning a new interface, building a new habit, or breaking an existing flow. Dwell works through the one behavior every human already performs — reading a screen. The activation energy for adoption is as close to zero as software can get. The first time someone hovers over an error code and Dwell tells them what it means, the product sells itself.

Development runs every Saturday and Sunday. Months 1–4
Foundation

Android C++ engine + Dwell Lens, Veil, Focus

Build the C++ accessibility engine for Android using the Accessibility Service via JNI bridge. Integrate llama.cpp with SmolLM2 135M and Qwen3 1.7B quantized models. Build the RAM-detection router that assigns the device tier at startup. Implement Dwell Lens, Dwell Veil, and Dwell Focus — the three features that require no subscription and ship to every user. Test across a range of Android devices: Tecno, Infinix, Samsung A-series, flagship devices. By the end of this phase, the core Dwell experience is working reliably on Android.

Months 4–7
Pro Features I

Dwell Compass, Dwell Memory, Dwell Pulse

Build the behavioral logging infrastructure that underlies both Compass and Memory. Implement the Compass objective function translation, real-time scoring, and daily alignment curve. Build Dwell Memory's session logging, synthesis pipeline, and WhatsApp export. Build Dwell Pulse with the initial past question database seeded from publicly available WAEC, JAMB, and NECO papers. Semantic similarity matching using a bundled sentence transformer. Community upload pipeline for Phase 2 database growth.

Months 7–10
Pro Features II

Dwell Forge, Dwell Search

Build the Dwell Forge reading session tracker, 12-minute retrieval prompt system, Forge response generation, and pattern tracking across sessions. This is the most intellectually demanding feature to tune — the quality of the Forge response must be genuinely useful, not generically correct. Build Dwell Search: Brave Search API integration, local synthesis pipeline, source citation, and browser handoff. Add transparent disclosure about the search query leaving the device.

Months 10–14
Windows + Polish

Windows port and cross-platform stabilization

Port the C++ core engine to Windows using the Win32 API and low-level mouse hooks. All feature logic lives in C and is identical across platforms — only the OS accessibility layer changes. Polish the overlay UI across both platforms. Address edge cases in content extraction — canvas elements, non-standard UI components, PDF viewers. Build the Paystack integration for Nigerian subscribers and Stripe for global users.

Months 14–18
Beta

Closed campus beta at four universities

Beta launch with campus representatives at UNILAG, OAU, UI, and FUTA. Dwell Veil leads the onboarding — it demonstrates value and earns the accessibility permission before any other feature runs. Real usage data collected. Dwell Forge and Dwell Compass receive the most tuning attention based on actual user behavior. Critical issues resolved before public launch. Payment infrastructure live.
